The Area

Monaco Solicitors is an award-winning UK employment law firm specialising exclusively in employee representation. We are driven by a mission to help individuals resolve workplace disputes without going to court and are proud of our innovative legal tech and fully remote working culture. Our focus is on supporting access to justice and providing meaningful change for our clients.

The Role

As a South Africa-based lawyer, you will join our team to support specialist UK employment lawyers on a variety of matters, particularly those under our Access to Justice Scheme. In your daily work, you will handle new enquiries, assist with eligibility assessments, and use the latest AI and legal technology to manage workflows and draft documents. This role offers you the opportunity to gain UK legal experience while working in a collaborative, 100% remote environment.

Responsibilities

  • Case Management: Handle new client enquiries and assist with eligibility assessments; support UK solicitors with legal research, correspondence, and negotiations.
  • Legal Drafting: Help draft legal documents, including 'without prejudice' letters, schedules of loss, and employment tribunal claim forms.
  • Tech Integration: Use AI and internal legal tech to improve efficiency and manage workflows.
  • Communication: Communicate with clients and/or respondents under supervision; participate in regular remote team meetings and legal training.
  • Administrative Support: Undertake legal admin work and participate in project work following established processes.

Qualifications

  • Experience: Previous experience as a lawyer dealing with contentious work; experience in legal drafting, legal research, and negotiation.
  • Technical Proficiency: Demonstrable tech-savviness, specifically including the use of AI tools to improve efficiency.
  • Communication Skills: Excellent written and spoken English with professional communication skills.
  • Work Status & Logistics: Must have self-employed status and the ability to work full-time remotely with a reliable and proactive work ethic.
  • Demonstrated Behaviors: You show a passion for access to justice; you are a diligent and reliable team player with excellent self-organisation and attention to detail.
  • Preferred: Case handling strategy and case work experience are preferred but not necessary.

Pay and Structure

  • Employment Type: Full-time, self-employed.
  • Remote Work: 100% remote with remote onboarding and training provided.
  • Growth: Strong performers may have future growth opportunities within the team.
  • Culture: Supportive and inclusive team culture with regular virtual collaboration.
